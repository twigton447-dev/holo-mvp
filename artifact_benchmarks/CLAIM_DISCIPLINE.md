# Artifact Benchmark Claim Discipline

This benchmark is designed to support a serious claim, not a demo claim.

## Core Claim

Given the same frozen context, same brief, same turn budget, same output requirements, same model family access, and same blinded scoring process, a Governor-orchestrated adversarial multi-model loop can produce stronger complex artifacts than solo recursive model runs.

The claim is about artifact quality under controlled conditions. It is not a claim that Holo is always better, that every route wins, or that the system is production-ready in any regulated domain.

## What We Are Testing

- Whether recursive adversarial review improves artifact quality over solo iteration.
- Whether Gov mission packets preserve accumulated learning and force targeted repair.
- Whether deterministic validity gates prevent high-specificity but incomplete artifacts from winning.
- Whether multiple models together discover implementation, compliance, risk, and source-grounding failures that single models miss.
- Whether the effect holds across frontier 3-model, frontier 2-model, and mini 3-model cohorts.
- Whether the effect repeats across multiple artifact-producing domains.

## Public-Ready Claims Require

- Frozen source packet and brief.
- Hash-locked packet files and runner files.
- Same role sequence and turn budget across Holo and solo conditions.
- Same final word band and required sections.
- Same blinded judge packet format.
- Three independent blinded judges for the scored result.
- Deterministic validity-gate report for final artifacts.
- Judge consistency checks and any contradiction flags disclosed internally.
- Token and latency accounting.
- Trace preservation sufficient for autopsy.
- Clear label that the result is an experiment unless a benchmark-credit gate is explicitly passed.

## Public Claims To Avoid

- "Holo always beats frontier models."
- "Holo is objectively smarter than GPT/Claude/Gemini."
- "Holo is production-ready for trading, medical, legal, or cyber decisions."
- "The system can trade, diagnose, provide legal advice, or operate infrastructure."
- "Judges proved Holo is better" without stating packet, cohort, judges, score method, and limitations.
- Any claim based on a run with invalid artifacts, unscored artifacts, non-blinded judges, missing traces, or unresolved score contradictions.

## Safe External Framing

Use language like:

- "In this controlled artifact benchmark..."
- "Using the same frozen context and six-turn budget..."
- "Against the same model solo baselines..."
- "Blinded judges scored the final artifacts..."
- "Holo showed uplift on implementation realism, hidden-failure detection, and client usefulness..."
- "This is not investment, medical, legal, cyber, or operational advice."

## Internal-Only Evidence

Keep these internal unless explicitly approved for release:

- Raw provider prompts.
- Raw traces with full artifacts.
- Sealed anonymization maps.
- Judge identity-to-provider mappings before public packaging.
- Failed or diagnostic runs that may confuse the final claim.
- Private packet source packs before client-safe review.

## Required Autopsy Before Any Strong Claim

Every serious result needs a full autopsy:

- Packet hash and runner hash match the lock.
- All expected conditions completed.
- Final artifacts exist, are non-empty, and pass the validity gate.
- Word counts are in band or the exception is clearly marked.
- No internal process residue in final artifacts.
- Unknown source IDs are absent.
- Brief-required disclaimers and required items are present.
- Judge rows include all criteria.
- Judge score/verdict contradictions are separated from clean rows.
- Clean-only and all-judge scores are both reported.
- Holo gain/loss is reported by criterion, not only by total score.
- Trace progression explains where Holo gained or degraded.
- Token/latency totals are recorded.

## Benchmark Credit

Default status is `benchmark_credit=false`.

Only promote to benchmark-credit candidate when the run is complete, hash-locked, fully scored, autopsied, and reviewed. Public claim approval is a separate step.
