**Board Strategy Memo: AI Infrastructure Capacity Commitment**

**To:** Board of Directors and CFO  
**From:** Management  
**Subject:** Two-Year AI Infrastructure Capacity Contract — Recommendation and Decision Framework  
**Classification:** Board Confidential

## Recommendation

**Do not sign the full two-year reserved AI infrastructure contract now. Instead, preserve optionality for a structured six-month validation window and return to the board only if explicit prerequisite gates and supporting triggers are met.**

This recommendation balances runway risk against commercial pressure. The $19.2M two-year obligation equals roughly 46% of the company’s $31M cash. Finance has stated that full commitment could compress runway from 24 months to 15 months if growth slows. At the same time, Sales attributes $6M of at-risk expansion pipeline to AI feature reliability, and enterprise customers are requesting data residency, audit logs, and latency commitments. Gross margin has already declined from 73% to 66% over three quarters as inference usage outpaced pricing changes.

Waiting six months at expected utilization carries an estimated incremental on-demand premium of approximately $1.68M. This is the explicit price of resolving material uncertainties before locking in a $19.2M fixed cost. Future reservation availability and pricing are unknown; the company is not preserving a guaranteed option but avoiding an irreversible commitment while evidence gaps remain.

## Why Six Months

Six months is the minimum period required to complete three sequential workstreams with defensible evidence:

- Root-cause diagnosis of reliability incidents under current on-demand conditions (30–60 days).  
- Deal-level re-qualification of the $6M at-risk pipeline (60–90 days).  
- Assessment of whether pricing or usage-mix changes can stabilize gross margin independent of reservation (90–180 days).

Three months is insufficient to finish all three. Twelve months is unnecessary if evidence is clear by month six. The $1.68M expected premium is accepted as the cost of disciplined validation.

## Cost of Waiting

Reserved capacity costs $9.6M per year. On-demand costs approximately 1.35× that amount at expected utilization, or roughly $12.96M per year. The annual premium is therefore approximately $3.36M. Over six months the central estimate of the incremental cost is $1.68M. Demand volatility means the realized premium could be lower or higher; the board should treat $1.68M as the expected-utilization benchmark.

## Explicit Assumptions

1. The linkage between reserved capacity and the reliability issues affecting the $6M pipeline is unverified.  
2. Future reservation pricing and availability at $9.6M per year are not confirmed.  
3. The $6M pipeline figure reflects Sales attribution and has not been validated deal-by-deal.  
4. On-demand capacity remains available during the window at the stated 1.35× multiple.  
5. Growth remains uncertain; the 18% YoY ARR trajectory on $42M base is the current run rate but not guaranteed.  
6. No intermediate contract structures are described in the available context.

## Decision Criteria (Trigger Framework)

Return to the board for commitment approval only if both prerequisite gates are satisfied and at least two of the three supporting triggers are met.

**Prerequisite Gates (both required):**  
1. Root cause confirmed with evidence: capacity constraints are shown, via incident data and utilization logs, to be the primary driver of reliability issues cited by enterprise customers or correlated with pipeline risk. Partial or ambiguous findings do not satisfy this gate.  
2. Downside runway acceptable: CFO confirms in writing that runway remains above a board-approved minimum under a defined growth-slowdown scenario after the full commitment.

**Supporting Triggers (at least two required):**  
3. The $6M pipeline is re-qualified at the deal level with documented evidence that specific opportunities depend on reserved-capacity reliability improvements.  
4. A material share of active enterprise opportunities explicitly requires data residency, audit logs, or latency commitments that cannot be credibly delivered on on-demand infrastructure.  
5. Gross margin has not stabilized at 66% and management demonstrates, after isolating compute cost from pricing and usage-mix factors, that reservation would materially improve unit economics.

**Default posture at month six:** If both prerequisite gates are not met, the board does not commit. Management continues on-demand operation, presents a revised evidence plan, and returns only when gates are satisfied. Month six is not a forced decision point.

## Near-Term Action Plan

**Days 1–30**  
- CFO publishes a board dashboard tracking gross margin from the 66% baseline, AI-related reliability incidents, and the $6M pipeline as a tracked line item.  
- Map enterprise requirements across active opportunities and identify which can be met without reservation.  
- Inquire with the capacity provider on future availability and pricing of the reserved block.

**Days 30–90**  
- Complete root-cause analysis of reliability incidents against the evidence standard in Gate 1.  
- Re-qualify the $6M pipeline at the deal level.  
- Model gross margin trajectory under continued on-demand, separating compute cost from pricing drivers.

**Days 90–180**  
- Test whether pricing or usage-mix changes can arrest margin decline independent of reservation.  
- Conduct monthly board reviews against gate and trigger status.  
- At month six present a gate-and-trigger scorecard. Commit only if both gates and at least two triggers are met; otherwise apply the default on-demand posture.

The board is choosing between a $19.2M two-year fixed obligation and a six-month validation window that carries an expected $1.68M on-demand premium. The recommendation is to accept the premium to resolve the three central uncertainties before taking on the lock-in and runway risk.
