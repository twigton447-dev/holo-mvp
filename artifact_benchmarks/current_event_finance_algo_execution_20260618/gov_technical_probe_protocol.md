# HoloGov Technical Probe Protocol

Gov should not merely summarize. Gov must make the next model uncomfortable in the useful way: specific, technical, source-grounded, and hard to hand-wave.

## Standing Rule

Every mission packet must include pointed technical probe questions. The next model must either answer them in the artifact or explicitly resolve why they do not apply.

## Artifact Validity Rule

Incomplete artifacts are automatic failures. If a candidate final ends mid-sentence, misses required sections, lacks the required disclaimer, uses unknown source IDs, or carries internal mission-packet residue, Gov cannot select it for clean scoring.

Specificity is not enough. Gov must ask whether the artifact is technically rich and complete, readable, source-grounded, auditable, and client-ready.

Every mission packet must maintain a repair ledger:

- open issue -> repaired -> regressed -> still missing

For final selection, deterministic gates run before quality judgment. Gov compares Turn 5 and Turn 6 only after checking clean ending, word band, required brief items, process residue, source IDs, and disclaimers. If Turn 6 is richer but incomplete, Gov must select the complete valid candidate or require a surgical repair/merge and keep the run diagnostic until repaired.

Gov should preserve known winning finance features unless they violate source grounding or validity gates: intent provenance, funding formula, audit schema, tiered controls, and model-risk safeguards.

## Probe Categories

- Order flow and microstructure
- Benchmarks and peer comparison
- Portfolio weights and risk
- Funding, settlement, and clearing
- Regulatory controls and auditability
- Model risk and AI bias
- Client/practitioner credibility

## Examples

- What if VWAP improves while implementation shortfall worsens?
- Can peer-relative performance be a crowding warning rather than validation?
- Where does adverse selection after passive fills enter the decision?
- How does T+1 settlement affect cash availability and rebalance sequencing?
- What pre-trade controls fire before the model changes venue, schedule, or participation rate?
- What is the kill-switch condition and how is it audited?
- Would a head trader believe this, or does it sound like architecture theater?
- Could this sound technically rich while still being incomplete, non-client-ready, or unauditable?

The final judged artifact is selected after Turn 6 by Gov comparing Turn 5 and Turn 6. Turn 6 must not degrade Turn 5.
