# D2 Oil & Gas / Middle East JV Accounting Crisis Brief Task

## Crisis Frame

A fictional U.S.-linked public energy company holds a 35% equity-method interest in a Middle East oil and gas joint venture through a non-operated affiliate. A geopolitical energy disruption has created pressure around production continuity, distribution/repatriation, sanctions/export-control review, FX/cash realization, impairment sensitivity, and disclosure judgment.

Internal facts visible to you:

- JV carrying amount on the parent balance sheet is $480 million.
- A $75 million gross JV distribution has been locally approved but not remitted; the parent share is 35%.
- Gross JV local cash is reported by the operator at $120 million equivalent; the parent share is 35% before withholding, bank charges, and FX/repatriation effects.
- Gross production averaged 42,000 boe/day last quarter; current operator flash report says 38,600 boe/day.
- Regional shipping disruption has delayed two cargo liftings by 18 days, but cargoes are not cancelled in the packet.
- Local bank has delayed USD conversion/repatriation for the pending distribution while compliance documentation is refreshed.
- One regional service contractor on the JV vendor list has incomplete beneficial-ownership refresh. No SDN match is documented in the packet, but sanctions screening is not complete.
- Operations says pausing all JV spending could reduce production below 80% within 30 days. Legal says distribution release should wait for sanctions/export-control sign-off.
- Investor relations expects questions about Middle East exposure on the next earnings call.

Facts not provided:

- No complete JOA or PSA clause text.
- No legal opinion that sanctions prohibit the JV, distribution, bank, cargo, contractor, or commodity flow.
- No completed export-control classification for U.S.-origin equipment or technical services.
- No independent reserve-engineering update after the disruption.
- No recoverable-amount calculation, discount-rate update, or board-approved impairment conclusion.
- No confirmation that the pending distribution is legally trapped or permanently unrecoverable.

## Your Task

Write a 900-1,300 word decision-grade crisis brief for the CFO, audit committee, controller, general counsel, sanctions/export-control lead, treasury lead, regional operations lead, and investor-relations lead.

The brief must include:

1. Bottom-line recommendation.
2. What is happening and why it matters now.
3. Strongest evidence.
4. Weak, stale, limited, or conflicting evidence.
5. Calculations or data interpretation that matter.
6. Practical response options.
7. Risks of acting.
8. Risks of waiting.
9. Recommended next steps, including operational conditions or stop/go triggers.
10. Claim boundaries and disclaimer.

## Decision Space

You may recommend continuing with controls, pausing distributions/repatriation, escalating sanctions/export-control review, initiating impairment/reserve analysis, adding disclosure controls, renegotiating JV economics, or using a bounded conditional path. Do not assume regional risk proves a sanctions breach. Do not assume disruption automatically requires impairment. Do not treat FX/repatriation friction as realized loss without support. Do not invent JOA/PSA clauses, partner-default remedies, reserve reports, legal conclusions, or accounting conclusions absent from the packet.

Your brief should help leadership make a better oil-and-gas JV decision under uncertainty, not write a generic sanctions or impairment memo.
