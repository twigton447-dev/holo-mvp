# D3 Insurance / Reinsurance Catastrophe Risk Crisis Brief Task

## Crisis Frame

A fictional regional property insurer faces a July 1 catastrophe reinsurance renewal after severe catastrophe losses, affordability pressure, and uncertainty over flood/excluded-peril and aggregate-limit treatment. Leadership must decide whether to renew, reprice, restrict, buy more reinsurance, raise retention, reserve, disclose, escalate, or conditionally proceed.

Internal facts visible to you:

- The exposed book has 125,000 policies and $42 billion total insured value across hurricane, severe-convective-storm, wildfire-smoke, and flood-adjacent counties.
- Last 12 months gross catastrophe claims are $410 million; ceded recoveries booked to date are $235 million; retained net catastrophe loss is $175 million before late coverage disputes or development.
- Expiring treaty is $650 million excess of $150 million per occurrence, with reinstatement protection subject to aggregate terms not fully quoted in this packet.
- Renewal quote is $500 million excess of $250 million at $92 million annual premium, versus $78 million expiring premium.
- Statutory surplus at the start of the renewal decision is $1.10 billion.
- Board risk appetite says modeled retained loss from a 1-in-100 event should not exceed 30% of surplus without board risk-committee approval.
- Product team says full actuarial rate need points to an 18% average rate increase; state-facing affordability plan assumes no more than 8% average customer increase this year.
- Claims/legal teams report open disputes over whether some flood-driven losses fall outside homeowners coverage and whether annual aggregate terms limit recovery after multiple events.

Facts not provided:

- No full treaty wording or binding quote.
- No final actuarial indication memorandum.
- No state DOI approval or disapproval.
- No final reserve opinion, statutory filing, or auditor conclusion.
- No portfolio-level model output by ZIP, peril, or construction class.
- No legal coverage opinion on flood/exclusion or aggregate-limit disputes.
- No evidence that the insurer is insolvent or that reinsurance capacity is unavailable at any price.

## Your Task

Write a 900-1,300 word decision-grade crisis brief for the CEO, CFO, chief underwriting officer, chief actuary, claims lead, reinsurance broker, general counsel, investor-relations lead, and board risk committee.

The brief must include:

1. Bottom-line recommendation.
2. What is happening and why it matters now.
3. Strongest evidence.
4. Weak, stale, limited, or conflicting evidence.
5. Calculations or data interpretation that matter.
6. Practical response options.
7. Risks of acting.
8. Risks of waiting.
9. Recommended next steps, including operational conditions or stop/go triggers.
10. Claim boundaries and disclaimer.

## Decision Space

You may recommend renewing as quoted, buying additional protection, accepting higher retention, restricting underwriting, repricing, filing rate/deductible changes, pausing expansion, reserving, disclosure escalation, or a bounded conditional path. Do not assume broad catastrophe data proves this portfolio loss. Do not assume reinsurance hardening means insolvency. Do not resolve exclusions, aggregate limits, reserve adequacy, or rate approval without evidence.

Your brief should help leadership make a better catastrophe-risk decision under uncertainty, not write a generic climate-risk or insurance-market memo.
