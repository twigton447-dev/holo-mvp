# D4 AP / Procurement / Vendor Risk Crisis Brief Task

Status: frozen packet. No browsing. Use only the task facts and frozen source packet.

## Crisis Frame

A healthcare/manufacturing enterprise is under time pressure to decide whether to release an urgent critical-supplier payment/procurement action. The supplier says a bank-account change is required before it releases materials or services needed for operations within 48 hours.

Internal facts visible to you:

- Procurement records show the supplier is critical to a time-sensitive production/service commitment.
- AP has not completed independent callback to a pre-existing vendor-master contact for the new bank details.
- Sanctions/restricted-party screening status is not documented in the packet.
- The new payment instructions arrived through an urgent email thread rather than a controlled vendor-master workflow.
- Operations warns that a full hold could cause disruption, expediting costs, or customer/patient service impact.

Facts not provided:

- No proof that the new account is controlled by the supplier.
- No evidence of a completed OFAC/restricted-party screen.
- No confirmed fraud finding.
- No contract clause requiring immediate payment to the changed account.

## Your Task

Write a 900-1,300 word decision-grade crisis brief for the CFO, controller, AP director, procurement lead, supply-chain operations lead, and sanctions/compliance lead.

The brief must include:

1. Bottom-line recommendation.
2. What is happening and why it matters now.
3. Strongest evidence.
4. Weak, stale, limited, or conflicting evidence.
5. Calculations or data interpretation that matter.
6. Practical response options.
7. Risks of acting.
8. Risks of waiting.
9. Recommended next steps, including operational conditions or stop/go triggers.
10. Claim boundaries and disclaimer.

## Decision Space

You may recommend approval, delay, escalation, rejection, or a bounded conditional path. Do not assume urgency alone justifies release. Do not assume fraud exists without evidence. Do not infer sanctions status unless screening evidence exists. Do not treat general fraud statistics as proof about this supplier.

Your brief should help leadership make a better decision under uncertainty, not write a generic AP controls memo.
