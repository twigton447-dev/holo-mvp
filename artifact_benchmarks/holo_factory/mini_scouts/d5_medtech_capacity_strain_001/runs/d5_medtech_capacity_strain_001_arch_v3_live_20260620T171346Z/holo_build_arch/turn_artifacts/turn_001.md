## 1. WHAT IS HAPPENING (source-grounded)

A hospital system under respiratory and sepsis-related capacity strain is weighing rapid expansion of remote pulse-oximetry monitoring to keep lower-risk patients out of inpatient beds while detecting deterioration early (TASK_BRIEF). The frozen packet supplies regulatory context, one preprint, and consumer/secondary reporting — but **no packet source demonstrates that remote pulse-ox monitoring relieves hospital capacity**. That claim is unsupported and must remain unsupported (S9; TASK_BRIEF forbidden-overclaim list).

The regulatory posture is in motion but **not settled**:
- FDA's current public page tells patients **not to rely only on a pulse oximeter**; symptoms and worsening status matter, and accuracy concerns tied to skin pigmentation are flagged (S1).
- The FDA performance/labeling guidance is **explicitly Draft, not for implementation, and non-binding** (S2). This is the single most important boundary for synthesis: it is not approval or clearance.
- FDA press material says current evidence **suggests** accuracy differences between lighter and darker skin pigmentation, and the proposed recommendations aim to generate more representative studies (S4) — proposal language, not a finished standard.
- The Feb 2, 2024 advisory panel **discussed** study methods, data needs, labeling, and accuracy concerns in darker skin (S5) — discussion, not a binding decision.

## 2. WHY IT MATTERS NOW

Deterioration risk is real and severe: CDC reports at least 1.7 million U.S. adults develop sepsis yearly, with at least 350,000 dying during hospitalization or discharged to hospice (S3). This anchors the "risk of waiting" — the underlying clinical urgency is well-sourced even though the intervention's efficacy is not.

The tension synthesis must hold: **urgent need (S3) does not equal proven tool (S6, S7, S8 are weak/contradictory).**

## 3. EVIDENCE STRENGTH MAP (for synthesis author)

**Strong / authoritative (regulator + public-health):**
- Sepsis burden magnitude (S3).
- FDA's "do not rely only on the device; symptoms matter" caution (S1).
- Skin-pigmentation accuracy concern, repeated across S1, S2, S4, S5 — this is the most consistently corroborated factual thread in the packet.

**Weak / stale / contradictory / bounded (must be labeled as such):**
- **S6 (Davies arXiv 2020):** preprint, COVID-era, ear-canal vs finger breath-hold testing. Not current regulatory or adoption evidence; not peer-reviewed in-packet. Suggestive only.
- **S7 (Health.com 2022):** explicitly **contradictory** — one RCT found pulse oximetry no better than text check-ins; another view argued it catches silent hypoxia. This is the cleanest contradiction in the packet and must be surfaced, not averaged away.
- **S8 (Verywell 2024):** consumer explainer; notes less reliability on darker skin and that arterial blood gas is an invasive alternative. Useful for stakeholder communication, weak as evidence.
- **S2/S4/S5:** must NOT be upgraded to approval/clearance. Draft, proposal, and discussion respectively.

## 4. CALCULATION / INTERPRETATION FLAG

The packet provides **no efficacy rates, no triage-accuracy numbers, and no capacity-impact figures**. The only hard number is the sepsis burden (S3: ≥1.7M cases, ≥350,000 deaths). Synthesis author should resist constructing any quantitative capacity-relief estimate — there is no numerator/denominator in the packet to support one. S9 is a derived interpretive table, not independent data; it explicitly frames the adopt/pilot/delay/reject choice under uncertainty and separates what each source supports from what it does not.

**Equity calculation note:** if the device under-detects hypoxemia in darker-skinned patients (S1, S2, S4, S5), a remote-monitoring program that defers inpatient admission based on SpO2 readings could **systematically under-triage** that subgroup. This is a sourced, decision-relevant risk the final brief must carry.

## 5. THE FOUR OPTIONS — INITIAL FRAME

**ADOPT (rapid system-wide expansion):**
- Not supportable on this packet. No efficacy evidence (S6/S7 weak/contradictory), active accuracy concerns (S1/S2/S4/S5), no capacity-relief proof (S9). Recommend the synthesis author treat full adopt as the weakest option.

**PILOT (bounded, monitored deployment):**
- Most defensible given urgent need (S3) plus unsettled evidence. A pilot can pair device readings with symptom-based escalation (consistent with S1's "symptoms matter") and stratify outcomes by skin tone to address the equity gap (S2, S4, S5). Must not be framed as proof.

**DELAY (wait for stronger evidence / finalized guidance):**
- Defensible because FDA guidance is still Draft and non-binding (S2) and proposals are not final (S4). Risk of waiting = continued exposure to deterioration/sepsis burden (S3) with no triage augmentation.

**REJECT:**
- Hard to justify outright given S3 urgency, but supportable if equity under-triage risk (S1/S2/S4/S5) is judged unacceptable absent device-level accuracy assurance. Should be framed as conditional, not absolute.

## 7. RISKS OF WAITING (sourced)
