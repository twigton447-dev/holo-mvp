## D5 Remote Pulse-Ox Expansion Under Capacity Strain

**Role:** initial_decision_brief_drafter | **Turn:** 1 | **Status:** NOT the final artifact. Findings for synthesis author.

## 1. WHAT IS HAPPENING (source-anchored)

- The hospital system faces respiratory and sepsis-related capacity strain; leadership is weighing rapid expansion of remote pulse-oximetry to keep lower-risk patients out of inpatient beds while still catching deterioration early (TASK_BRIEF).
- Sepsis is the burden anchor: CDC describes it as a life-threatening emergency, with **at least 1.7M U.S. adults/year** and **at least 350,000 adults** dying during hospitalization or discharged to hospice (S3_CDC_SEPSIS_CONTEXT_2026).
- The regulatory environment is **in motion, not settled**: FDA has issued a *draft* guidance and a *press release* proposing updated recommendations on pulse-ox performance across skin tones (S2_FDA_DRAFT_GUIDANCE_2025; S4_FDA_PRESS_RELEASE_2025), preceded by a Feb 2024 advisory panel discussion (S5_FDA_ADVISORY_MEETING_2024).

## 2. WHY IT MATTERS NOW

- The decision lever is triage/bed-conservation under acute strain (TASK_BRIEF) against a high-mortality deterioration condition (S3). The "now" is real.
- But the central pressure point: **the packet does NOT contain evidence that remote pulse-ox solves or relieves capacity strain.** S9 explicitly frames the task as a decision under uncertainty (adopt/pilot/delay/reject), not a proven solution. The synthesis author must NOT let urgency convert into an overclaim.

## 3. STRONG EVIDENCE (what holds)

- **Sepsis burden / clinical stakes** — authoritative, current-dated public-health source (S3).
- **FDA-acknowledged accuracy concern by skin pigmentation** — corroborated across FOUR independent FDA artifacts: the product page (S1), draft guidance rationale (S2), press release stating current scientific evidence suggests accuracy differences between lighter and darker pigmentation (S4), and advisory discussion (S5). This convergence is the strongest signal in the packet — but note it cuts AGAINST naive deployment, not for it.
- **FDA's own usage caveat**: patients should not rely only on a pulse oximeter; symptoms and worsening status matter (S1). This is strong, current, and directly limits any "remote-monitoring-as-safety-net" framing.

## 4. WEAK / STALE / CONTRADICTORY / SUGGESTIVE EVIDENCE (must be bounded)

- **S6 (Davies ear-ox preprint, 2020):** older COVID-era *preprint*; reports faster response from ear-canal vs finger site in breath-hold testing. NOT peer-reviewed, NOT current, NOT adoption evidence. Bound as suggestive only. Provenance flag in-source.
- **S7 (Health.com, 2022):** secondary journalism reporting *directly conflicting* findings — one RCT found pulse oximetry **no better than text check-ins**, another viewpoint argued it detects silent hypoxia. This is the key contradiction: it undercuts the assumption that remote SpO2 adds triage value over cheaper follow-up.
- **S8 (Verywell, 2024):** consumer explainer; reiterates darker-skin unreliability and notes arterial blood gas is an invasive alternative. Useful for stakeholder communication, weak as evidence.
- **Equity hazard:** the pigmentation accuracy gap (S1/S2/S4/S5/S8) means rapid expansion could **systematically under-detect deterioration in darker-skinned patients** — turning a capacity tool into a disparity vector. This belongs in risks-of-acting.

## 5. CALCULATIONS / DATA INTERPRETATION THAT MATTER

- **No quantitative effect size is available in the packet** for remote pulse-ox triage performance. The synthesis author should state this explicitly rather than manufacture numbers.
- S3 provides the only hard figures (1.7M; 350,000). These size the *problem*, not the *intervention benefit*. Do not divide one by the other to imply impact.
- S7 contains a directional but unquantified comparison (pulse-ox ≈ text check-ins in one RCT). Treat as qualitative; no n, no CI, no setting in the excerpt.
- **Interpretive caution:** S9 is a *derived* artifact (no independent URL) built from S1–S8. It must not be cited as an independent corroborating source — it is a framing/interpretation aid only.

## 6. THE FOUR OPTIONS — initial frame for synthesis

| Option | Supporting pull | Constraining pull |
|---|---|---|
| **ADOPT (rapid, system-wide)** | Acute strain + high sepsis stakes (S3, TASK_BRIEF) | No packet evidence of capacity relief; contradicted triage value (S7); equity accuracy gap (S1/S2/S4/S5); FDA "don't rely only on it" (S1). Weakest-supported option. |
| **PILOT (bounded, instrumented)** | Lets deterioration-detection signal be tested locally; aligns with "symptoms also matter" safety net (S1); contains equity monitoring | Requires guardrails; does not itself relieve strain near-term |
| **DELAY** | FDA guidance still draft/non-binding (S2); evidence conflicting (S7) | Capacity strain and sepsis mortality are present-tense (S3); inaction has cost |
| **REJECT** | Conflicting evidence (S7), accuracy concerns (S1–S8) | Forgoes a plausible adjunct; not packet-justified as fully harmful either |

**Drafter's read (for synthesis, not final):** The packet most defensibly supports a **bounded PILOT with explicit equity safeguards and clinical-symptom backstop**, NOT rapid system-wide adoption. The evidence base is too thin and too conflicted to support "adopt," and too clinically urgent to clearly support "reject" or open-ended "delay." Synthesis author should weigh but is not bound.

## 7. RISKS OF ACTING (rapid adoption)

- Missed deterioration via known pigmentation inaccuracy → disparate harm (S1, S2, S4, S5, S8).
- Over-reliance on a device FDA says should not be relied on alone (S1).
- Deploying on contested triage value (S7) → false reassurance, possible delayed escalation.
- Regulatory exposure: building on *draft, non-binding* guidance (S2) treated as if settled.

## 8. RISKS OF WAITING

- Present-tense sepsis mortality and capacity strain continue (S3, TASK_BRIEF).
- Lost opportunity to generate the system's own local evidence under controlled conditions.

## 9. PRACTICAL NEXT STEPS (frame only)

1. Scope a small instrumented pilot with predefined deterioration/escalation criteria anchored to symptoms, not SpO2 alone (S1).
2. Build equity stratification (skin-tone–disaggregated accuracy/outcome tracking) into any pilot from day one (S1/S2/S4/S5/S8).
3. Track FDA guidance status; treat S2 as draft/non-binding until finalized (S2).
4. Pair any monitoring with a non-device follow-up arm to test the S7 "no better than text check-ins" contradiction locally.
