## 1. THE OPTIONS MENU MUST BE FOUR REAL OPTIONS — NOT A DISGUISED YES

The brief must present adopt / pilot / delay / reject as genuinely distinct, each with a decision rule (S9_DERIVED_EVIDENCE_PRESSURE_TABLE; TASK_BRIEF). Weakness to flag for synthesis:

- **"Adopt (rapid full expansion)"** is not supportable on this packet. There is zero packet evidence linking remote pulse-ox to reduced admissions or safe bed conservation (TASK_BRIEF; S9), and the only trial-level signal says pulse oximetry was "no better than text check-ins" (S7_HEALTH_HOME_PULSE_OX_CONFLICT_2022). A synthesis that lists "adopt" without flagging it as **unsupported by the packet** would smuggle the capacity-relief overclaim back in. Adopt should be presented and then explicitly bounded as not justified by current evidence.
- **"Pilot (controlled, instrumented)"** is the only option the packet can rationally support, and only as a *hypothesis test*, not a rollout (S9; S7). The brief must say the pilot tests whether remote SpO2 adds triage value over cheaper check-ins — because S7 shows that comparison can come out negative.
- **"Delay"** and **"Reject"** must each have a trigger condition. Delay pending finalized FDA guidance is defensible because S2 is explicitly draft/non-binding (S2_FDA_DRAFT_GUIDANCE_2025). Reject-for-primary-safety-net use is defensible because FDA says patients should not rely only on a pulse oximeter (S1_FDA_PULSE_OX_PAGE_2025).

**Synthesis directive:** Differentiate options by *use-case*, not just intensity. "Reject as a sole sepsis safety net" can coexist with "pilot as an adjunct with mandatory symptom escalation." Collapsing these loses decision value.

## 2. OPERATING GATES — WHAT MUST BE TRUE BEFORE ANY DEPLOYMENT

For leadership usefulness, the brief should convert evidence limits into **go/no-go gates**. Each gate must trace to a source:

- **Gate A — No sole-reliance.** Any deployment must pair SpO2 with symptom/worsening-status escalation pathways; SpO2 alone is insufficient per FDA (S1_FDA_PULSE_OX_PAGE_2025). A pilot without a symptom-escalation arm should be a no-go.
- **Gate B — Skin-tone performance safeguard.** Because accuracy concerns across skin pigmentation are the strongest cross-source signal (S1; S4_FDA_PRESS_RELEASE_2025; S5_FDA_ADVISORY_MEETING_2024; S8_VERYWELL_LIMITED_EXPLAINER_2024), any pilot must stratify monitoring and escalation thresholds and track outcomes by patient subgroup. Without this, the system knowingly scales a tool with documented differential-accuracy concerns.
- **Gate C — Endpoint pre-commitment.** Because S7 shows pulse-ox can fail to beat text check-ins, the pilot must pre-register what "success" means (admissions avoided safely, missed-deterioration rate, escalation latency) before launch — or the result is uninterpretable.
- **Gate D — Regulatory-status honesty.** No procurement or labeling language may describe FDA material as approval, clearance, or a finalized standard (S2 is draft/non-binding; S4 is a press release; S5 is advisory discussion). This directly closes the Turn-2 audit defect.

**Synthesis directive:** Present these as binary gates, not aspirations. Leadership under capacity pressure needs "do not launch unless X," not "consider X."

## 3. RISK SYMMETRY — ACTING vs. WAITING MUST BOTH BE HONEST

The brief must not stack only the risks of acting (which would covertly recommend reject) nor only the risks of waiting (which would covertly recommend adopt). Both columns need real entries:

**Risks of acting (rapid expansion):**
- False reassurance / missed silent hypoxia, especially in darker-skinned patients (S1; S4; S8) — a patient-safety and liability exposure.
- Resource diversion into monitoring that may not yield bed availability (S7; TASK_BRIEF) — opportunity cost during the very strain you're trying to relieve.
- Using pulse-ox as a primary sepsis safety net contradicts FDA usage guidance for a 1.7M-cases/350,000-deaths condition (S1; S3_CDC_SEPSIS_CONTEXT_2026).

**Risks of waiting (delay/reject):**
- Continued inpatient burden under genuine respiratory and sepsis strain (TASK_BRIEF; S3) — the "now" is real and the status quo is not free.
- Forgoing a *potential* adjunct triage signal that some viewpoints argue detects silent hypoxia (S7) — bounded as contested, not proven.

**Synthesis directive:** The honest finding is that the strongest evidence (skin-tone accuracy concern, FDA sole-reliance caveat, the negative S7 trial arm) weights **against rapid full adoption** but does **not** mandate outright rejection of a gated pilot. State this asymmetry plainly rather than hiding it.

## 4. CALCULATION / INTERPRETATION THAT IS ACTUALLY USABLE

There is no packet dataset enabling a quantitative capacity model — and the brief must not fabricate one. The defensible interpretive moves:

- **Stakes framing only:** sepsis volume (≥1.7M/yr) and mortality (≥350,000) establish that *missed* deterioration is high-consequence (S3) — this sizes the downside of a false-negative, not the benefit of monitoring.
- **Equity exposure framing:** the differential-accuracy concern means false-negative risk is *not uniformly distributed* across the patient population (S1; S4; S8). For an institution, that is a concentrated, foreseeable liability — usable for risk-committee framing.
- **Comparator framing:** S7's "no better than text check-ins" is the single most decision-relevant data point because it sets the bar a pilot must clear (S7).

**Synthesis directive:** Label all of this as interpretation of qualitative source claims, not computed capacity relief. Do not imply a numeric bed-savings estimate exists.

## 5. USEFULNESS-UNDER-PRESSURE CHECK

For a CMO/regulatory/ops/quality audience deciding fast, the brief is only useful if it:
- gives a **default recommendation** (gated pilot as adjunct, not rapid expansion) with the evidence reason attached;
- gives **stop conditions** (Gates A–D) leadership can hand to implementation;
- states what would **change the decision** (finalized FDA guidance per S2; pilot endpoints met per S7-style comparison);
- never lets urgency (S3) substitute for evidence of effect (TASK_BRIEF; S9).

**Weakness to avoid:** a brief that is evidence-correct but ends without an actionable default. Under capacity pressure, "it depends" is an operational failure. The synthesis must land a bounded default plus next steps.
