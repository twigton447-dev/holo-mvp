# Task Brief: D5 MedTech / Hospital Capacity Crisis

You are writing for healthcare and MedTech leadership: the CMO, regulatory lead, clinical operations lead, quality lead, and implementation/product leadership.

The hospital system is under respiratory and sepsis-related capacity strain. Leadership is considering rapid expansion of remote pulse-oximetry monitoring to keep lower-risk patients out of inpatient beds while still detecting deterioration early.

Write a 900-1,300 word decision-grade crisis brief. The brief must help leadership decide whether to adopt, pilot, delay, or reject rapid expansion of remote pulse-ox monitoring under the evidence limits in the source packet.

Use only the frozen packet sources. Do not browse. Cite source IDs for every factual claim. Distinguish what the evidence strongly supports from what is weak, stale, contradictory, or only suggestive.

Your brief must cover:

- what is happening;
- why it matters now;
- strong evidence;
- weak, stale, or contradictory evidence;
- calculations or data interpretation that matter;
- practical response options;
- risks of acting;
- risks of waiting;
- practical next steps;
- claim boundaries and disclaimer.

Required disclaimer:

This is a benchmark artifact and not medical advice, regulatory advice, clinical protocol approval, statistical certification, product clearance, or legal advice.

Do not claim that remote pulse-ox monitoring solves hospital capacity strain unless the packet sources support that claim. Do not overstate FDA draft guidance, advisory discussion, or press material as approval or clearance. Do not treat older COVID-era technical claims, consumer health reporting, or manufacturer-adjacent comments as current clinical proof.
