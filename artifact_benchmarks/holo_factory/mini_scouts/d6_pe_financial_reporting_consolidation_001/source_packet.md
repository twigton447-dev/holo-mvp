# Frozen Source Packet: D6 PE / Financial Reporting & Consolidation

## Crisis Frame
PE-backed financial-close, consolidation, lender-reporting, and covenant-action risk after acquisition activity.

## Decision Question
Should leadership certify and send the close package and lender covenant certificate now, delay for corrections, disclose a covenant issue, or escalate finance/legal/audit review before action?

## Case Facts

- Company profile: A fictional PE-backed healthcare services platform, AtlasCare Holdings, has completed four acquisitions in nine months and must deliver a year-end consolidation package and lender covenant certificate within 48 hours.
- Decision time: CFO signoff is requested by 18:00 ET tomorrow so the sponsor can release the lender reporting package before a revolver draw and board update.
- Proposed action: Certify the consolidated close package and adjusted EBITDA schedule used for a first-lien net leverage covenant and lender reporting package.

### Close Package Status
- The controller says the checklist is 96 percent complete and all required workbook tabs are populated.
- The acquisition accounting memo for the largest acquisition is still marked management draft.
- Treasury says the delayed lender package could complicate a planned $25 million revolver draw next week.

### Acquisition And Reporting Facts
- Target Delta closed on November 18; management included Target Delta October-December EBITDA in lender adjusted EBITDA even though only post-acquisition operations are consolidated in GAAP results.
- Parent sold $8.4 million of services and supplies to Target Delta after close; $2.1 million gross profit remains in acquired inventory and work-in-process at year-end.
- A $6.5 million receivable/payable mismatch remains between parent and Target Delta ledgers after preliminary intercompany elimination.
- Management added back $12.0 million of annualized purchasing synergies, $4.0 million of projected staffing reductions, and $3.0 million of former-owner compensation normalization.
- The lender covenant model permits pro forma cost savings only when supported by a management memo, expected within 18 months, not duplicative, and capped at 20 percent of covenant EBITDA unless lender consent is obtained.
- Net debt for the covenant package is $440.0 million. Reported adjusted EBITDA is $80.0 million, producing reported net leverage of 5.50x against a maximum covenant of 5.50x.

### Not Provided
- No signed acquisition accounting memo for Target Delta.
- No completed intercompany reconciliation tying both ledgers to source invoices and inventory quantities.
- No lender consent for add-backs above the covenant cap.
- No auditor signoff on opening balance-sheet fair values or synergy add-back support.
- No board approval to submit a covenant package that includes unresolved intercompany and add-back items.
- No final purchase agreement schedule proving whether former-owner compensation is recurring, transition-service, or discontinued.

### Calculations To Check
- Reported net leverage is $440.0 million net debt divided by $80.0 million adjusted EBITDA, or 5.50x.
- The proposed $19.0 million add-back package is 23.75 percent of reported adjusted EBITDA before removing it from the denominator, above the stated 20 percent cap unless lender consent exists.
- If the unsupported portion of add-backs is limited to the 20 percent cap, maximum permitted add-backs are $16.0 million on the reported EBITDA base, leaving $3.0 million unsupported before other issues.
- If the full $19.0 million add-back package and the $2.1 million unrealized intercompany profit are removed, adjusted EBITDA falls to $58.9 million and net leverage rises to about 7.47x.
- If only the excess add-back above the 20 percent cap ($3.0 million), the $2.1 million unrealized intercompany profit, and the $6.5 million unreconciled intercompany mismatch are treated as unresolved, at-risk EBITDA support is $11.6 million; adjusted EBITDA at risk is $68.4 million and net leverage is about 6.43x.
