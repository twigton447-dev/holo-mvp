# D6 Task Brief: PE Financial Reporting & Consolidation Crisis Action Brief

You are writing a decision-grade crisis/action brief for finance leadership at a PE-backed platform company.

Use only the frozen case facts and source packet. Do not browse. Do not invent missing lender language, auditor conclusions, board approvals, acquisition-accounting results, or intercompany reconciliation evidence.

## Crisis Frame

PE-backed financial-close, consolidation, lender-reporting, and covenant-action risk after acquisition activity.

## Decision Question

Should leadership certify and send the close package and lender covenant certificate now, delay for corrections, disclose a covenant issue, or escalate finance/legal/audit review before action?

## Required Artifact

Write a 900-1,300 word main-body decision brief. Target 1,100 words. The brief must help leadership decide whether to certify and submit the close package/lender covenant certificate now, delay for targeted repairs, submit with exclusions or reservations, seek lender consent, or escalate finance/legal/audit review.

## Required Sections

1. Bottom line recommendation.
2. What is happening and why it matters now.
3. Strongest evidence.
4. Weak, stale, or conflicting evidence.
5. Calculations or data interpretation.
6. Practical response options.
7. Risks of acting.
8. Risks of waiting.
9. Recommended next steps.
10. Claim boundaries / disclaimer.

## Minimum Decision Quality Bar

A strong brief separates GAAP consolidation discipline, lender covenant definitions, close checklist status, and management add-back support. It should show the leverage/add-back arithmetic, identify unresolved intercompany and stub-period risks, compare action options, and define concrete stop/go triggers for certification, lender submission, consent, and escalation.

Do not approve or block solely because the close checklist looks procedurally complete. Do not overclaim public sources. Preserve uncertainty where the packet lacks the actual lender agreement, auditor signoff, board approval, or signed acquisition accounting memo.
