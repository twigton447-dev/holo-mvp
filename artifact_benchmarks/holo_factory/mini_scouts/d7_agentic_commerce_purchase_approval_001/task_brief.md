# D7 Task Brief: Agentic Commerce / Autonomous Purchase Approval Action Brief

You are writing a decision-grade action-boundary brief for procurement, operations, safety, and compliance leadership.

Use only the frozen case facts and source packet. Do not browse. Do not invent missing EHS approvals, vendor-screening results, budget approvals, restricted-party clearance, substitute-SKU validation, or facility storage authorization.

## Crisis Frame

Autonomous purchasing approval under urgency, with unresolved safety, authorization, vendor, sanctions, budget, substitution, and scope gates.

## Decision Question

Should leadership allow, block, escalate, conditionally approve, split, substitute, or modify an autonomous purchase order before execution?

## Required Artifact

Write a 900-1,300 word main-body decision brief. Target 1,100 words. The brief must help leadership decide whether to allow autonomous order release, block it, escalate it, conditionally approve a narrower purchase, split to a safer source, or modify the order before execution.

## Required Sections

1. Bottom line recommendation.
2. What is happening and why it matters now.
3. Strongest evidence.
4. Weak, stale, or conflicting evidence.
5. Calculations or data interpretation.
6. Practical response options.
7. Risks of acting.
8. Risks of waiting.
9. Recommended next steps.
10. Claim boundaries / disclaimer.

## Minimum Decision Quality Bar

A strong brief separates operational urgency from execution authorization. It identifies unresolved purchase gates, distinguishes vendor/product claims from authoritative controls, shows the inventory, purchase-order aggregation, and downtime arithmetic, and defines concrete stop/go triggers for autonomous release, manual approval, conditional release, and escalation.

Do not approve just because the purchasing workflow appears procedurally complete. Do not block generically without recognizing operational cost. Preserve uncertainty where the packet lacks EHS release, vendor-screening completion, budget-owner approval, and substitute-SKU validation.
