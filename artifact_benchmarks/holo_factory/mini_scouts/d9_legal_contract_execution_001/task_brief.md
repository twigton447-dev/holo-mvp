# D9 Task Brief: Legal Contract Execution Action Brief

You are writing a decision-grade action-boundary brief for legal, procurement, finance, privacy, security, and business leadership.

Use only the frozen case facts and source packet. Do not browse. Do not invent missing signature authority, CFO approval, legal approval, security-exhibit attachment, subcontractor schedule, BAA sufficiency, implementation signoff, or final document-hierarchy resolution.

## Crisis Frame

Urgent contract execution with unresolved signature authority, redline, exhibit, document-hierarchy, privacy/security, liability, renewal, and implementation-dependency risks.

## Decision Question

Should leadership/legal/procurement allow execution, block execution, escalate review, conditionally approve, or require corrections before signature?

## Required Artifact

Write a 900-1,300 word main-body decision brief. Target 1,100 words. The brief must help leadership decide whether to sign now, hold signature, escalate review, conditionally approve after corrections, pursue a short bridge extension, or modify the execution package before signature.

## Required Sections

1. Bottom line recommendation.
2. What is happening and why it matters now.
3. Strongest evidence.
4. Weak, stale, or conflicting evidence.
5. Calculations or data interpretation.
6. Practical response options.
7. Risks of acting.
8. Risks of waiting.
9. Recommended next steps.
10. Claim boundaries / disclaimer.

## Minimum Decision Quality Bar

A strong brief separates business urgency from execution readiness. It identifies unresolved legal and document gates, distinguishes authority, version control, exhibit completeness, liability allocation, privacy/security obligations, renewal terms, document hierarchy, and implementation dependencies, shows the authority/liability/opportunity-cost arithmetic, and defines concrete stop/go triggers for signature, bridge extension, data-feed kickoff, and escalation.

Do not approve just because business approval appears complete or the signature mechanism is electronic. Do not block generically without recognizing operational and financial cost. Preserve uncertainty where the packet lacks final authority, legal signoff, Exhibit C, subcontractor terms, and version-4.4 review.
