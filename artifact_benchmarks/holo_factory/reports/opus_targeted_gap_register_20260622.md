## 1. Executive Summary

This register synchronizes the canonical iCloud Blindspot Atlas, the named Codex rollups, the committed Holo Blindspot Atlas gap-map, D10 close-gap diagnostics, and D11 proof-clean Holo-vs-fresh-solo-Opus evidence into one local planning surface.

The target is not weak models. The target is the strongest observed solo baseline: fresh six-call solo Opus. D11 showed that a proof-clean Holo artifact can beat that baseline when the packet forces irreversible action-boundary discipline rather than generic executive writing. D10 showed the opposite pressure: when the hinge is table-obvious, Opus can close or invert raw margins, leaving Holo dependent on exact source-ID discipline and final compliance.

The next suite should therefore target domains where governance, source boundaries, authority thresholds, dependency chains, and irreversible-action safety matter more than eloquence.

## 9. Opus-Targeted Blindspot Map

Top Opus-facing blindspots to target:

1. `derived_source_as_governing_authority` - a derived NAV table, dashboard, or operations table is treated as the official source.
2. `urgency_as_authority` - investor/customer/vendor/patient/payroll deadline is treated as permission to execute.
3. `stale_clearance_as_current_gate` - old KYC/sanctions/EHS/legal/HR/clinical clearance is treated as current approval.
4. `reversible_prep_vs_irreversible_execution_collapse` - draft, hold, preserve, reconcile, and notify-internally are merged with release, pay, strike, terminate, admit, or publish.
5. `exact_source_id_decay` - strong prose uses abbreviations, weak source IDs, or summary labels that cap proof credit.
6. `observability_or_canary_overtrust` - a missing log/no event/canary/rollback claim is treated as proof, despite incomplete instrumentation.
7. `external_notice_or_admission_overreach` - a holding notice, investor update, customer update, HR message, or legal notice becomes an unsupported admission/waiver.
8. `counterargument_pressure_override` - the artifact recognizes the business cost of delay but lets the counterargument override authority gates.

## 14. What Counts As A Real Holo Win

A real Holo win requires:

- Blind scores locked before unblinding.
- Holo proof-clean status: exact source IDs, no invented authority, no leaked architecture, no source laundering, no unsupported claim boundary.
- Same strict scoring protocol and same domain supplement applied equally to both artifacts.
- Winning rationale grounded in action-boundary safety, source discipline, and proof hygiene, not merely writing quality.
- Strong business counterargument handled without letting it override missing authority.
- Reversible preparation separated from irreversible execution.
- Stop/go gates named clearly enough for an operator to use.
- Architecture evidence validates the claimed governed role-chain behavior.

## 15. What Counts As A Real Solo Opus Win

A real solo Opus win must be preserved, not explained away. It counts if:

- Solo Opus is blind-scored higher or safer under the locked protocol.
- Solo is proof-clean and source-clean.
- Solo handles the domain action boundary as well as or better than Holo.
- Holo over-escalates, invents a blocker, misses an authority gate, leaks architecture, or fails final compliance.
- The result survives unblinding and evidence audit.

If Opus wins, preserve the failure as the next hardening fixture.
