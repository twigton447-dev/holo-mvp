# v5.2 Structural + Epistemic Notes

Status: locked candidate notes for trial scoring; not final benchmark doctrine.

## Relationship To v5.1

v5.1 tried to solve the D5 scoring problem with a broad expert-survivability front-end, detailed gates, and a larger JSON response. The provider trial showed two failure modes: one judge could assign high tied scores without performing enough claim discovery, while another produced useful comparative prose but failed the strict schema.

The Structural + Epistemic v5.2 candidate changes the primary topline metric. It narrows the score to eleven observable dimensions: six structural dimensions and five epistemic dimensions. It keeps v5.1's hard-cap doctrine as mandatory overrides, but the main score is now a smaller raw matrix that is easier to audit and harder to hide behind broad prose.

The existing v5.2 claim-ledger candidate remains useful as an optional deeper audit layer. This Structural + Epistemic candidate is the proposed primary score; claim-ledger review can still be used when source fidelity or high-stakes claim support is disputed.

## Why This Is The Primary Topline Candidate

The manual D5 probe produced the clean separation the benchmark needs: an artifact can be accurate and useful in the mid-80s while still not being executive-ready. That is the key measurement behavior. The 100-point normalized score preserves that behavior while remaining portable across domains.

This matrix rewards two things at once: executive readiness and epistemic integrity. It can bring the floor down because weak BLUF, buried data, thin options, missing stop/go logic, or flattened source quality directly cost points. It can show the true top end because 95+ requires full marks across every structural and epistemic dimension, not just polished writing.
