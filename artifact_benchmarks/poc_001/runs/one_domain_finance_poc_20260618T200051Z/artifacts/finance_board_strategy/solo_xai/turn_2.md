**Evidence and Risk Critique – Turn 2 Draft**

**Unsupported claims**  
The draft states that on-demand capacity “may prove insufficient for data-residency or latency SLAs.” No fact in the source context supports or refutes SLA capability on on-demand; the only statement is that enterprise customers are asking for those commitments. This is an inference, not evidence.  

The claim that “on-demand spend exceeds 1.35× reserved pricing for three consecutive months at greater than 70% of expected volume” triggers a staged commitment introduces a 70% volume threshold that does not exist in the provided facts.  

**Missing nuance / weak evidence use**  
- The $6M at-risk pipeline is treated as directly convertible or lost based solely on capacity choice. The source only says “Sales attributes $6M … to AI feature reliability,” not that reserved capacity is the sole remedy or that on-demand cannot meet reliability needs.  
- Runway compression to 15 months is presented as a binary outcome of “full commitment.” The Finance warning is conditional on growth slowing; the draft does not surface the trigger (growth rate) or quantify how much slowing produces the 15-month outcome.  
- Gross-margin trajectory (73% → 66%) is cited but never linked quantitatively to the $9.6M annual reservation cost or to the 1.35× on-demand premium.  

**Hidden risks and failure modes**  
- Two-year lock-in is listed in the risk register yet the action plan only asks the provider for a six-month deferral clause; no fallback if the provider refuses is defined.  
- The three decision criteria contain no downside trigger for cash preservation (e.g., ARR growth falling below 18% or cash falling below a stated floor). This leaves the “preserve optionality” recommendation exposed if pipeline conversion stalls early.  
- Contingency trigger requires “two of the three criteria breached” before an ad-hoc Board call; this creates a timing lag that could still force a rushed decision inside the six-month window.  
- Pure cost framing trap is called out in the conclusion but the decision criteria remain entirely quantitative (pipeline %, spend multiple, runway months) with no qualitative criterion for enterprise credibility.
