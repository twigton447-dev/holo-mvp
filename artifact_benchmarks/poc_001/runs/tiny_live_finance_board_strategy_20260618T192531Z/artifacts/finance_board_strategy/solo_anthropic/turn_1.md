# AI Infrastructure Capacity Contract: Board Strategy Memo

**To:** Board of Directors and Chief Financial Officer
**From:** Strategy and Finance
**Date:** [Current Quarter]
**Classification:** Board Confidential
**Re:** Two-Year Reserved AI Capacity Contract — Commit Now vs. Preserve Optionality for Six Months

---

## Executive Summary

This memo recommends a **staged partial commitment**: execute a reserved capacity contract at approximately 50% of the offered volume now, with a structured six-month option window to evaluate whether to expand to full commitment. This approach defends the $6 million at-risk expansion pipeline and addresses enterprise credibility requirements while limiting incremental fixed-cost exposure and preserving adequate operating runway. A bare full commitment or a bare deferral each carry unacceptable asymmetric risk given current financial and market conditions.

---

## Explicit Assumptions

The following assumptions underpin this analysis. The board should revisit the recommendation if any assumption proves materially incorrect.

1. **Revenue defense is the primary decision variable.** The $6 million at-risk expansion pipeline is treated as a real and near-term revenue risk, not a speculative sales estimate. If the sales team's attribution is overstated, the calculus shifts toward deferral.

2. **On-demand cost premium is durable.** On-demand capacity is assumed to cost approximately 1.35× the reserved price at expected utilization for the foreseeable contract period. If cloud provider pricing shifts materially downward, the cost-savings rationale weakens.

3. **Gross margin pressure is structural, not transient.** The decline from 73% to 66% over three quarters reflects a durable mismatch between inference cost growth and pricing. This memo does not assume the company can reprice customers quickly enough to offset infrastructure costs without reliability improvements.

4. **18% ARR growth continues at base case.** Finance's runway calculation of 15 months under full commitment assumes growth slows. The base case assumes 18% growth continues, which partially offsets the cash consumption of the contract. Sensitivity to a growth slowdown is addressed in the risk register.

5. **A partial commitment is contractually available.** This memo assumes the cloud provider will negotiate a partial reservation. If the contract is all-or-nothing at $9.6 million per year, the decision framework in Section 5 applies with modified thresholds.

6. **No additional debt or equity financing is assumed.** The company has $31 million in cash and no debt. This memo does not model a capital raise as a mitigant, though the board may wish to consider that optionality separately.

## Situation Assessment

The company sits at a strategic inflection point where infrastructure decisions are no longer purely operational — they are directly tied to enterprise revenue retention and expansion.

**Financial position:** $31 million in cash, no debt, $42 million ARR growing at 18% year over year. At current burn, Finance estimates 24 months of operating runway. A full two-year commitment at $9.6 million annually adds approximately $19.2 million in fixed obligations, compressing runway to 15 months under a growth-slowdown scenario.

**Margin trajectory:** Gross margin has declined 7 percentage points over three quarters, from 73% to 66%. This is a material signal. Inference usage is scaling faster than the company's ability to reprice or optimize. Reserved capacity at a lower unit cost is one lever to arrest this decline; it is not the only lever, but it is the most immediately available one.

**Revenue at risk:** The sales team attributes $6 million of expansion pipeline to AI feature reliability concerns. Enterprise customers are specifically requesting data residency, audit logs, and latency commitments before expanding. These are not vague preferences — they are procurement-stage requirements. Failure to meet them does not merely slow expansion; it risks losing contracts to competitors who can make those commitments.

**The core tension:** Committing improves reliability and enterprise credibility but increases fixed costs during a period of volatile demand. Waiting preserves cash optionality but may allow the $6 million pipeline to erode and signals to enterprise buyers that the company cannot make durable infrastructure commitments.

## Decision Criteria

The board should use the following criteria and thresholds to govern both the initial partial commitment and the six-month expansion decision.

**Criteria for proceeding with partial commitment now:**

- At least $3 million of the $6 million at-risk pipeline requires a credible infrastructure commitment within the next 90 days to remain in active evaluation. *(Confirm with sales leadership.)*
- Partial commitment annual cost does not reduce operating runway below 20 months at base-case growth.
- Cloud provider accepts partial reservation terms without punitive pricing on the reserved tranche.

**Criteria for expanding to full commitment at the six-month review:**

- ARR growth rate has held at or above 15% for two consecutive quarters.
- At least $4 million of the $6 million at-risk pipeline has converted or is in final contract stages.
- Gross margin has stabilized or improved from the 66% baseline.
- Inference utilization on the partial reserved capacity has reached 70% or above, validating demand assumptions.
