## Board Strategy Memo: AI Infrastructure Capacity Decision

**To:** Board of Directors, Chief Financial Officer
**From:** [Your Name/Department]
**Date:** October 26, 2023
**Subject:** Recommendation on AI Infrastructure Capacity Commitment

**Executive Summary:**

This memo recommends that the company **preserve optionality for six months** regarding a two-year AI infrastructure capacity contract. While a commitment now offers potential cost savings and addresses immediate customer demands for reliability, the associated financial risks, particularly the reduction in runway and the current volatility in inference costs, outweigh the benefits at this juncture. A six-month deferral allows for greater clarity on growth trajectory, pricing strategy refinement, and a more accurate assessment of future AI infrastructure needs, while mitigating the risk of locking into a significant fixed cost.

**1. Introduction:**

Our company is at a critical juncture regarding its AI infrastructure strategy. As AI features become increasingly integral to our SaaS offering, ensuring reliable and performant infrastructure is paramount. We are faced with a decision: commit to a two-year reserved AI accelerator capacity contract now, or maintain flexibility by preserving optionality for six months. This decision has significant implications for our financial health, competitive positioning, and ability to meet evolving customer expectations.

**2. Current Situation Analysis:**

*   **Financial Health:** The company is in a strong financial position with $31 million in cash and no debt. Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) stands at $42 million, growing at a healthy 18 percent year-over-year.
*   **Cost Pressures:** Gross margin has declined from 73 percent to 66 percent over the past three quarters. This erosion is directly attributed to inference usage increasing at a faster rate than our ability to adjust pricing.
*   **Customer Demands:** Enterprise customers are increasingly requesting critical service level agreements (SLAs), including data residency, audit logs, and latency commitments. These demands are directly tied to the reliability and performance of our AI infrastructure.
*   **Sales Impact:** The sales team attributes $6 million of at-risk expansion pipeline to concerns around AI feature reliability. Addressing this is crucial for continued growth.
*   **Infrastructure Options & Costs:**
    *   **Reserved Capacity:** A two-year commitment for reserved accelerator capacity costs $9.6 million per year.
    *   **On-Demand Capacity:** On-demand capacity is estimated to cost roughly 1.35 times the reserved price at expected utilization levels. However, demand for this capacity is volatile.
*   **Financial Risk of Commitment:** Finance has cautioned that a full commitment to the reserved capacity could reduce our runway from 24 months to 15 months if growth slows.

**3. Decision Criteria:**

The decision to commit to AI infrastructure capacity will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

*   **Financial Runway:** Maintaining a healthy financial runway is critical for operational stability and future investment.
*   **Customer Commitments:** Ability to meet enterprise customer demands for reliability, data residency, audit logs, and latency.
*   **Revenue Protection & Expansion:** Safeguarding existing revenue and enabling expansion pipeline growth by ensuring AI feature reliability.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Achieving the most cost-effective solution for AI infrastructure needs, considering current and projected utilization.
*   **Strategic Flexibility:** Retaining the ability to adapt to evolving market conditions, technological advancements, and internal strategic shifts.

**4. Staged Options and Trigger Thresholds:**

We propose a staged approach to managing AI infrastructure capacity:

*   **Option A: Preserve Optionality (Recommended for the next 6 months)**
    *   **Description:** Continue utilizing on-demand capacity for AI inference, while actively monitoring usage patterns, cost drivers, and customer feedback. Simultaneously, engage in deeper analysis of pricing elasticity and potential for AI feature-specific pricing adjustments.
    *   **Trigger Thresholds for Re-evaluation (within 6 months):**
        *   **Significant Increase in On-Demand Costs:** If the average monthly cost of on-demand AI infrastructure exceeds $1.2 million (representing a sustained 1.35x multiplier on the reserved cost, adjusted for current utilization estimates), we will re-evaluate a reserved commitment.
        *   **Loss of Key Enterprise Deals:** If we lose more than $2 million in ARR from the $6 million at-risk expansion pipeline specifically due to AI feature reliability concerns, we will accelerate the decision to secure reserved capacity.
        *   **Clearer Pricing Model for AI Features:** If we finalize and implement a pricing model that demonstrably covers the increased inference costs and improves gross margins, we will have better data to assess the long-term cost of capacity.
        *   **Stabilized Growth Trajectory:** If our ARR growth rate consistently exceeds 18% for two consecutive quarters, providing greater confidence in our financial projections.

*   **Option B: Commit to Two-Year Reserved Capacity (Considered after 6 months or if triggers are met)**
    *   **Description:** Enter into the $9.6 million per year, two-year reserved AI accelerator capacity contract.
    *   **Trigger Thresholds for Commitment (if Option A is pursued):**
        *   **Sustained High Utilization:** If on-demand usage consistently indicates a need for capacity equivalent to or exceeding the reserved amount for more than three consecutive months.
        *   **Critical Customer Commitments:** If a significant enterprise customer (representing >$5M ARR) requires firm latency and reliability commitments that can only be guaranteed through reserved capacity.
        *   **Improved Gross Margins:** If our gross margins stabilize or improve to above 70% through pricing adjustments, making the fixed cost of reserved capacity more manageable.
