## Executive Summary

This memo evaluates whether the company should execute a two-year reserved AI infrastructure capacity contract at $9.6M per year or delay commitment by six months to preserve financial optionality. **The recommendation is a staged commitment: execute a partial reserved capacity contract now, structured to cover confirmed baseline demand, while preserving optionality on the remaining capacity tranche pending defined trigger thresholds over the next two quarters.**

A full immediate commitment is not recommended. A full deferral is equally inadvisable. The rationale, assumptions, risks, and action plan follow.

---

## Situation Assessment

The company is at an inflection point where AI infrastructure is transitioning from a product differentiator to a revenue-critical dependency. Three dynamics are converging simultaneously:

**Margin compression is accelerating.** Gross margin has declined from 73% to 66% over three quarters as inference usage has outpaced pricing adjustments. This is not a one-time event — it reflects a structural cost shift that will worsen under on-demand pricing if utilization continues to grow.

**Pipeline is at risk.** The sales team attributes $6M of expansion pipeline to AI feature reliability. Enterprise customers are explicitly requesting data residency, audit logs, and latency commitments — requirements that are difficult to fulfill without predictable, reserved infrastructure. Failing to address these requirements does not merely slow growth; it risks losing deals already in motion.

**Cash position constrains full commitment.** With $31M in cash, no debt, and a 24-month runway at current burn, a full two-year commitment of $19.2M total ($9.6M × 2) would reduce runway to approximately 15 months if growth slows — a materially different risk profile that limits the board's ability to respond to adverse scenarios.

---

## Explicit Assumptions

The following assumptions underpin this analysis. The board should revisit the recommendation if any assumption proves materially incorrect.

1. **On-demand pricing remains at approximately 1.35× reserved pricing** at expected utilization levels. If on-demand costs fall materially, the economic case for reservation weakens.
2. **The $6M at-risk pipeline has a reasonable probability of conversion** if reliability commitments are met. This memo does not assign a specific probability — that judgment belongs to the CRO — but treats the pipeline as real and material.
3. **ARR growth of 18% YoY continues at or near current rates** for the planning horizon. Finance's warning about runway reduction is predicated on a growth slowdown scenario, which is treated here as a tail risk, not a base case.
4. **Inference usage growth continues to outpace pricing adjustments** in the near term, sustaining margin pressure under on-demand pricing.
5. **A partial reservation structure is commercially available.** This memo assumes the vendor will negotiate a phased or tiered commitment. If the vendor requires full commitment as a condition of reserved pricing, the decision calculus changes and the board should revisit this memo.
6. **No additional debt or equity financing is assumed** within the planning window.

## Decision Criteria

The board should use the following criteria to evaluate the staged commitment and the trigger thresholds for the second tranche:

**Commit the first tranche now if:**
- Gross margin is at or below 66% and on-demand pricing remains at ≥1.35× reserved rates
- At least one enterprise deal in the $6M pipeline has explicitly conditioned expansion on reliability or latency commitments
- Cash balance remains above $25M after accounting for the first-year reserved cost

**Activate the second tranche (expand reservation) if, within six months:**
- ARR growth holds at ≥15% YoY through the next two reporting periods
- Gross margin stabilizes or improves as a result of reserved pricing
- The $6M pipeline converts at ≥50% (i.e., ≥$3M in closed expansion ARR attributable to AI reliability)
- Cash balance remains above $22M

**Defer or abandon the second tranche if:**
- ARR growth falls below 12% YoY
- Cash balance approaches $20M without a clear path to improvement
- On-demand pricing falls to ≤1.15× reserved rates, reducing the economic incentive for reservation

## Strategic Framing: Why Neither Extreme Is Correct

**The case against full immediate commitment** is not primarily about cost — it is about optionality value under uncertainty. A 15-month runway is not a comfortable operating position for a board managing through a period of AI infrastructure volatility. If the market shifts, if a key customer churns, or if a financing event is needed, the company's negotiating position is materially weaker with compressed runway. The two-year lock-in also deserves explicit acknowledgment: the AI infrastructure landscape is evolving rapidly, and committing fully to a specific vendor and capacity configuration for 24 months carries technology risk that is difficult to quantify with available information.

**The case against full deferral** is equally clear. Waiting six months while competitors potentially offer reliability guarantees to the same enterprise prospects in the $6M pipeline is not a neutral act. Margin compression under on-demand pricing will continue to erode gross margin below 66% if utilization grows as expected. The cost of waiting is not zero — it is measured in margin points and pipeline risk.
