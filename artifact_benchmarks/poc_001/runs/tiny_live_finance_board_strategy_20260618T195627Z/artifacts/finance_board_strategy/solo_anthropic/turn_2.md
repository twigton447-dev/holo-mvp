# Evidence and Risk Critique: Board Strategy Memo (Turn 2 of 6)

---

## Overall Assessment

The draft is structurally sound and avoids the most obvious traps (pure cost framing, ignoring lock-in). However, it contains several unsupported claims, underexplored risks, and analytical gaps that would expose the memo to legitimate challenge from a rigorous CFO or board member. The following critique is organized by category with concrete repair instructions.

---

## 1. Unsupported or Overstated Claims

**"This is not a one-time event — it reflects a structural cost shift."**
*Problem:* The source context establishes that margin fell from 73% to 66% over three quarters. It does not confirm the cause is permanent or structural. Inference usage growing faster than pricing changes is a stated fact, but whether pricing adjustments will eventually catch up is unknown. Calling this "structural" is an interpretive leap beyond the evidence.
*Repair:* Replace with: "Whether this compression is permanent or correctable through pricing adjustments is uncertain; however, the three-quarter trend warrants treating it as durable for planning purposes until evidence suggests otherwise."

**"Failing to address these requirements does not merely slow growth; it risks losing deals already in motion."**
*Problem:* The context states enterprise customers are *asking* for these features and that sales *attributes* $6M of pipeline to AI reliability. It does not confirm deals will be lost without them, nor that competitors are offering equivalent guarantees. This is presented as fact when it is a sales-team assertion.
*Repair:* Qualify explicitly: "Sales attributes $6M of expansion pipeline to AI feature reliability. This attribution has not been independently validated and reflects sales-team judgment, not confirmed customer commitments. The board should request CRO verification before treating this figure as certain."

**"A 15-month runway is not a comfortable operating position."**
*Problem:* True directionally, but the memo does not define what runway threshold the board considers acceptable, nor does it acknowledge that 15 months is Finance's *warning scenario* (growth slows), not the base case. Presenting it without this context overstates the immediate risk.
*Repair:* Clarify: "Finance's 15-month runway estimate is conditioned on a growth slowdown scenario. Under the base case of 18% ARR growth, runway compression would be less severe — though the exact figure is not calculable from available data. The board should request Finance to model runway under both base and downside cases before the decision meeting."

## 2. Missing Nuance and Hidden Risks

**The partial commitment assumption is load-bearing but unverified.**
The entire recommendation hinges on the vendor offering a partial or phased structure. This is listed as Assumption 5 but treated as a minor caveat. If the vendor requires full commitment as a condition of reserved pricing — a common practice in accelerator capacity markets — the staged approach collapses entirely.
*Repair:* Elevate this to a pre-condition, not an assumption. Add to the action plan: "Before this memo is tabled for board vote, the CFO and procurement lead must obtain written confirmation from the vendor that a partial reservation structure is available at reserved pricing. If unavailable, this memo requires revision."

**The 1.35× on-demand premium is described as applying "at expected utilization" — but utilization is volatile.**
The source context explicitly notes demand is volatile. If utilization is lower than expected, on-demand costs may not reach 1.35× reserved rates, weakening the economic case for reservation. If utilization spikes, on-demand costs could exceed 1.35×. Neither tail is modeled.
*Repair:* Add a sensitivity note: "The 1.35× on-demand premium applies at expected utilization. At lower utilization, the premium may not materialize, reducing the cost savings from reservation. At higher utilization, the premium could exceed 1.35×, strengthening the case. The board should request a utilization sensitivity table from Finance."

**Two-year technology lock-in risk is acknowledged but not quantified or actioned.**
The memo notes the AI infrastructure landscape is "evolving rapidly" but provides no criteria for what would constitute a stranded-asset scenario, nor any contractual mitigation (e.g., exit clauses, capacity resale rights).
*Repair:* Add to the risk register: "Negotiate contract terms that include capacity resale rights or early termination provisions, even at a penalty. Quantify the penalty cost relative to the full lock-in exposure before signing."

**Data residency and audit log requirements are noted but not connected to the infrastructure decision.**
Enterprise customers are requesting data residency and audit logs. It is not established in the source context that reserved capacity — as opposed to on-demand capacity — is what enables these features. The link between capacity type and compliance capability is assumed, not demonstrated.
*Repair:* Add a flagged uncertainty: "It is assumed that reserved capacity enables the data residency and latency commitments enterprise customers require. If these features depend on vendor configuration or software controls rather than capacity reservation type, the reliability argument for reservation weakens. Engineering confirmation is required."

## 3. Decision Criteria Weaknesses

**The $22M and $20M cash floor thresholds appear without derivation.**
The memo sets trigger thresholds at $22M and $20M cash balances but provides no explanation for why these specific figures represent acceptable floors. A CFO will immediately ask how these were derived.
*Repair:* Either derive them from burn rate and minimum operating reserve calculations (using available data), or explicitly label them as illustrative: "These thresholds are illustrative. Finance should confirm minimum operating cash reserve requirements before the board adopts them as binding triggers."

**The ≥50% pipeline conversion threshold ($3M) is reasonable but its timeframe is ambiguous.**
The trigger says "within six months" but does not specify whether $3M must be *closed* or merely *advanced to late stage*.
*Repair:* Specify: "≥$3M in closed-won expansion ARR with signed contracts, not pipeline stage advancement, within six months of first-tranche execution."
