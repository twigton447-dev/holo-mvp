**Board Strategy Memo: AI Infrastructure Capacity Decision**

**To:** Board and CFO  
**From:** Strategy Team  
**Date:** [Current]  
**Subject:** Commit to 2-Year Reserved Capacity Now or Preserve Optionality

**Recommendation**  
Do not commit to the $9.6M/year reserved contract now. Preserve optionality for six months. Proceed with on-demand capacity while establishing trigger-based review points. This protects the 24-month runway (currently supported by $31M cash, zero debt) against the risk of runway compression to 15 months if growth slows.

**Explicit Assumptions**  
- ARR of $42M continues at 18% YoY; gross margin remains pressured at 66% due to inference growth.  
- On-demand pricing stays at 1.35× reserved at expected utilization, with demand volatility unchanged.  
- The $6M at-risk expansion pipeline stays tied to AI feature reliability.  
- Enterprise requirements for data residency, audit logs, and latency cannot be fully met without reserved capacity.  
- No material change in cash position or debt status over the next six months.

**Risk Register**  
- **Runway compression (High):** Full commitment locks in $19.2M over two years, reducing flexibility if ARR growth decelerates.  
- **Pipeline erosion (Medium-High):** Delaying may weaken the $6M expansion opportunities if reliability SLAs cannot be demonstrated.  
- **Margin deterioration (Medium):** Continued on-demand usage sustains gross-margin pressure at 66%.  
- **Lock-in trap (High):** Two-year term removes ability to adjust for utilization volatility or pricing shifts.  
- **Execution gap (Medium):** Enterprise asks for residency and latency commitments remain unaddressed without reserved capacity.

**Decision Criteria**  
1. Runway threshold: Maintain ≥18 months cash runway.  
2. Pipeline trigger: If >$3M of the $6M at-risk ARR shows clear loss due to reliability within six months, escalate to partial commitment.  
3. Margin trigger: If gross margin falls below 64%, reassess on-demand economics.  
4. Utilization visibility: Require three consecutive months of demand data before any reserved purchase.

**Staged Options & Near-Term Action Plan (Next 6 Months)**  
- **Option A (Preferred):** Continue on-demand; allocate up to $1.5M incremental spend for targeted reliability improvements (latency, audit logging) to protect pipeline.  
- **Option B:** Pilot 6-month partial reservation covering 40% of expected needs if pipeline trigger is hit.  
- **Option C:** Full reservation only after runway and utilization criteria are both satisfied.

**Immediate Actions**  
1. Finance to model monthly cash runway under on-demand scenarios (Week 1–2).  
2. Sales to quantify pipeline at risk with specific reliability gaps (Week 2–3).  
3. Engineering to deliver costed plan for data-residency and latency features using current infrastructure (Week 3–4).  
4. Schedule board review at month 6 with trigger dashboard.

This approach avoids pure cost framing and two-year lock-in while retaining flexibility.
