# Packet Roadmap

Status: planning, not frozen, not benchmark credit.
Date: 2026-06-19

## Immediate Principle

Do not run every idea at once.

Build packets in an order that proves the story cleanly:

1. HoloBuild can generate better artifacts.
2. HoloBuild can improve already-good artifacts.
3. Holo can find discrepancies in trusted artifacts.
4. HoloVerify can validate facts and boundaries.
5. HoloGov ties the two worlds together with evidence, repair, and final-selection discipline.

## Packet Queue

### Packet 001: Finance Blank-Slate Build

Status: built and hash-locked.

Family: blank-slate build.

Question:

Can Holo produce a better institutional algorithmic-execution report than solo frontier or mini models under identical source context?

Why first:

It is already the closest to complete and has the strongest current evidence trail.

Next action:

Run fresh current-lock mini route, analyze, and roll up mean lift.

### Packet 002: Finance Best-Existing-Artifact Improvement

Status: next build candidate.

Family: best-existing-artifact improvement.

Question:

Given an already strong finance report, can Holo find hidden weaknesses and produce a better version than solo improvement?

Why it matters:

This is closer to client reality. Clients often bring a memo, deck, model, or policy that is already good.

Baseline options:

- best solo frontier finance artifact
- best existing Holo finance artifact from prior diagnostic run
- human-curated finance memo
- public institutional report excerpt, if source/licensing allows summary-based use

Scoring:

- baseline versus solo-improved
- baseline versus Holo-improved
- solo-improved versus Holo-improved
- hidden failures found and repaired

### Packet 003: Finance Discrepancy Discovery

Status: next build candidate.

Family: discrepancy discovery.

Question:

Can Holo find material discrepancies, missing assumptions, unsupported claims, or uncoded risks in an already trusted execution policy/report?

Why it matters:

This is the insurance story. Holo protects users by finding what trusted work missed.

Finding schema:

- artifact claim
- source or authority conflict
- unsupported inference
- severity
- business consequence
- repair recommendation
- false-positive risk

### Packet 004: Known-Outcome Finance Retrospective

Status: research candidate.

Family: known-outcome retrospective.

Question:

Could Holo have identified a real finance failure mode from pre-event evidence before the outcome was known?

Examples:

- public forecast miss
- liquidity or execution failure
- risk-control failure later described in a postmortem or enforcement action
- strategy memo contradicted by later market behavior

Boundary:

The generation packet must contain only pre-event information. The outcome is used only for evaluation.

### Packet 005: Adversarial Paradox Reasoning

Status: skeleton exists.

Family: adversarial paradox reasoning.

Question:

Can Holo preserve contradictions and name impossible requirements instead of smoothing them into a plausible but wrong answer?

Why it matters:

This is the cleanest architecture test. It strips away domain prestige and tests whether the loop sees the trap.

Examples:

- improve speed, cost, quality, and safety with a fixed budget where all four cannot be maximized
- satisfy two policies that conflict
- optimize a metric that harms the real objective
- recommend action under missing authority

### Packet 006: Medical Diagnostic Paradox

Status: skeleton exists.

Family: medical diagnostic paradox.

Question:

Can Holo produce a safer synthetic diagnostic reasoning packet than solos by preserving uncertainty, red flags, and cannot-miss conditions?

Boundary:

Synthetic cases only. No real patient data. No medical advice. No diagnosis. No treatment plan.

Best first case type:

One common presentation with a dangerous alternate explanation, early normal tests, and one missing data point that changes the decision.

### Packet 007: Legal Competition / Court

Status: not scaffolded yet.

Family: competition/court.

Question:

Can Holo produce stronger adversarial legal reasoning, bench memos, or synthetic rulings than solo models under the same fact and authority pack?

Why it matters:

Law is naturally adversarial. Holo's architecture should shine where argument, counterargument, dissent, precedent, and administrability all matter.

Boundary:

Synthetic or historical/public fact patterns. Not legal advice. Not real-case adjudication.

### Packet 008: Cyber Known-Outcome Retrospective

Status: not scaffolded yet.

Family: known-outcome retrospective / discrepancy discovery.

Question:

Could Holo identify the incident-response failure mode from pre-incident or early-incident information before the public postmortem made it obvious?

Why it matters:

This bridges HoloBuild and HoloVerify. The artifact is a discrepancy/premortem packet; the scoring is grounded by later known facts.

### Packet 009: Hotel / Food Experience Quality

Status: not scaffolded yet.

Family: experience quality.

Question:

Can cheaper mini HoloAgents produce a measurably better guest or customer experience plan than solo minis?

Why it matters:

This makes the lift emotionally legible. People understand "better experience" immediately.

Examples:

- hotel service recovery packet
- restaurant menu and operations redesign
- travel itinerary under constraints
- student study and budget plan

### Packet 010: Student / College Workflow

Status: not scaffolded yet.

Family: experience quality / best-existing improvement.

Question:

Can mini HoloAgents help students produce better plans, essays, project proposals, study maps, or decision packets than solo mini models?

Why it matters:

This is a low-cost, high-accessibility lane. Mini HoloAgents may be good enough for many everyday users.

Boundary:

No misrepresentation, admissions fraud, or high-stakes professional advice.
