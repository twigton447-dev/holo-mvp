## The methodology claim

Most AI benchmarks pre-label ground truth before the models see the packet. That person's judgment could be wrong for the same reasons the model's judgment is wrong.

We don't do that. We run all 11 architectures blind, collect their reasoning chains, and derive the ground truth from the evidence. The Judge reads the packet plus all 11 reasoning chains and determines what the evidence actually supports.

You can't game a benchmark like this because the right answer doesn't exist when the models run. It only exists after.

That's worth a paragraph when you talk to institutional people. It's the credibility argument.
