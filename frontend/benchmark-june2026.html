Act I

The $184,750 Authorization Gap

The transaction packet appeared clean on the surface. The target vendor was valid. The banking route modification had been fully vetted through standard corporate security policies, including a portal submission with multi-factor authentication and a successful voice callback to the phone number on record since 2021. Two internal corporate approvers had signed off, and the vendor master file was updated prior to invoice arrival.

Based on this evidence, 10 out of 11 AI configurations approved the payment.

However, the invoice referenced a specific purchase order: PO FW-HFS-2026-04. The actual purchase order artifact was completely missing from the evaluation packet. Without that document, there was no verification of approved scope, amount ceiling, or formal procurement authorization.

The payment route was verified. The expenditure itself was completely unauthorized. Standard configurations cleared the loud risk. Holo Engine isolated the quiet gap.

Act II

Structural Friction vs. Weak Signals

The performance gap highlighted by this benchmark is not a function of raw model intelligence. Holo Engine ran the exact same frontier models utilized in the failing configurations. The variance in outcome is driven entirely by the structural shape of the orchestration framework.

The Breakdown of Multi-Agent Consensus

When faced with complex compliance packets, standard multi-agent frameworks routinely suffer from Consensus Collapse. In Condition C (Claude Sonnet Homogeneous Council), the framework established a Reviewer, an Evidence Checker, and a Skeptic.

Condition C - claude-sonnet-4-6 - Homogeneous Council - 4 turns - ALLOW (FAILED)
Reviewer: ALLOW - All four policy conditions met. BEC flag mitigated. Skeptic: ALLOW - I challenge but find no material gap. Every artifact is present. Evidence Checker: ALLOW - No inconsistencies found. Final Judge: ALLOW - Unanimous. No dissenting finding.

Because the superficial banking change controls were execution-perfect, the Skeptic role quickly folded. Without structural friction, the committee unanimously voted to let a simulated $184,750 proceed.

Governor: Models deadlocked on the unresolved authority gap. Execution halted. Verdict: ESCALATE.

Act IV

System Calibration and Constraints

  1. Domain Scope: This evaluation matrix specifically maps deterministic document and reference validation within Accounts Payable, Procurement, and Sanctions workflows. It is not currently calibrated for conversational context, fluid multi-party contract redlines, or long-horizon agentic commerce execution.
  2. False-Positive Target: By design, Holo Engine maximizes structural friction to eliminate false negatives at the action boundary. Based on our current development pipeline calibration, the system targets a projected unnecessary escalation rate of less than 3% across our upcoming 400-packet continuous integration stress test framework.
  3. Robustness Engineering: To verify reproducibility against model temperature variance, a 30-seed randomized stability testing framework for the core evaluation suite is currently pending final validation. Full stability logs will be committed to the open repository upon pipeline completion.
  4. Model Dependency: Holo Engine orchestrates logic and structural tension, but its capability baseline remains bound to the reasoning depth of the underlying frontier models (gpt-5.4, claude-sonnet-4-6, gemini-2.5-pro).
