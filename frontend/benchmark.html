Open Benchmark v4.0

Point-in-time architecture comparison for action-boundary adjudication.

Most AI benchmarks test general reasoning in a vacuum. This benchmark tests judgment at the action boundary: the final, irreversible moment before an AI agent wires money, provisions server access, or executes a financial roll-up.

Core Finding
Standalone frontier models suffer from Bidirectional Failure at this boundary:
  1. Procedural Obedience (False Negatives): They blindly approve mechanically clean but semantically flawed data.
  2. Contextual Brittleness (False Positives): They panic at valid business exceptions because they cannot extract resolving evidence from legal or operational documents.

The benchmark holds the model roster constant. It compares single-model judgment (GPT, Claude, Gemini) against Holo's multi-model adversarial council and constrained Governor layer.

Section 2 of 4 · Failure Mode Analysis

Failure Mode Analysis

When solo models failed the benchmark, the traces revealed three structurally distinct reasoning flaws. A single model cannot cover all three because the same reasoning loop that generates a concern is the loop that resolves it.

GPT-5.4
Detection Failure
The model accepts a plausible text narrative immediately and fails to cross-reference historical data that contradicts it. It mistakes a smooth explanation for factual truth.
Claude Sonnet 4.6
Persuasion Failure
The model correctly identifies the risk signal in an early turn, but talks itself out of it because the surrounding packet looks professional and clean.
All Solo Models
Contextual Brittleness
When presented with complex resolving evidence (e.g., a KPMG deal memo outlining a pending 90-day true-up), solo models panic. They misunderstand standard institutional mechanics and block perfectly valid transactions.

Holo's adversarial reactor prevents these failures by separating initial assessment from pressure testing. The Governor operates on verified evidence, not rhetorical persuasion.

Section 3 of 4 · Methodology & Integrity

Methodology & Integrity

This benchmark is designed as a crash-testing lab for AI actions, not a leaderboard.

Model Roster Parity

Solo baselines use the exact same model versions represented inside the Holo run. If Holo used stronger models than the solo baselines, the result would be confounded by model capability. The variable being tested is strictly adjudication architecture: isolated single-model judgment versus shared adversarial review.

Generous Solo Baselines

Solo frontier models were tested under highly generous conditions. They received the full evidence packet, policy framing, and decision context. They were not deprived of data. They failed because they lacked adversarial evidence collision.

A benchmark result is only published here if it passes six strict integrity gates:

1
Verdict Stability
Holds across randomized model and role assignment seeds.
2
Correct Catch Reason
The model cites the intended structural signal, not a random fluke.
3
No Answer Key in Context
No labeled field directly identifies the disqualifying condition.
4
Clean Trace
The turn-by-turn audit is readable without explanation.
5
One-Sentence Takeaway
The proof point is expressible in plain operational language.
6
No Infrastructure Contamination
No API timeouts or adapter failures affected the run.
Section 4 of 4 · Reproducibility & Future Work

Reproducibility & Future Work

Publicly Verifiable
Third parties can inspect scenario descriptions, payloads, solo-model conditions, and benchmark gates. Solo GPT, Claude, and Gemini conditions can be rerun against the published payloads using independent API keys.
Not Publicly Reproducible
The full Holo architecture is not open-source. The Governor logic, adversarial reactor configuration, model-routing details, turn heuristics, and verdict computation layer remain proprietary.
Controlled Review
For qualified evaluators, design partners, or technical reviewers, Holo can be run against held-out case files through a controlled black-box process. Contact us to submit novel payloads and review the resulting ALLOW / ESCALATE verdict and trace.
