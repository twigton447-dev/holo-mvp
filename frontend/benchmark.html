The bank routing information was correct and verified.

But the payment itself was never authorized.

The vendor was real. The bank details matched. The multi-factor authentication passed, and two company managers signed off. It looked completely normal.

But the invoice referenced a purchase order that didn't exist. Without that purchase order, there was no proof of approved spending limits or authority. Standard AI simply checked if the bank routing form was filled out correctly. Holo asked a harder question: Does this payment actually have permission to happen?

How It Works

Why Standard AI Fails

When normal AI models run into missing pieces of information, they tend to gloss over them. If an AI agent notes that a purchase order is missing, but sees that the banking form looks professional and complete, the system compromises. It prioritizes keeping the workflow moving over enforcing the safety rule.

Holo acts as a hard checkpoint. It explicitly isolates unverified or missing information. The central Governor blocks the AI from glossing over data gaps, freezing execution until the authority chain can be proved.

The Standard

The Ten-Gate Publication Process

To prevent cherry-picking or tuning our prompts to match the answer keys, every single test asset must clear ten distinct gates before it is published here. This ensures all results can be verified and reproduced from disk.

  1. Schema Validation
    The data structure is verified against native enterprise protocol definitions.
  2. Payload Scrubbing
    All tracking metadata, timestamps, and background markers are completely removed.
  3. Packet Freezing
    The immutable data packet is saved to disk and locked against future changes.
  4. Prompt Hash Generation
    The exact system instructions and prompt setups are hashed to keep the test context static.
  5. Blinded Trace Capture
    All evaluation models are executed at a locked, low temperature of 0.1 to limit randomness.
  6. Combined Hash Integration
    The packet data hash and prompt hash are merged into a single root signature to secure the run state.
  7. Independent Adjudication
    The isolated Holo Judge grades the final trace outputs against real company compliance targets.
  8. Behavior Labeling
    Models are automatically tagged based on whether they actually understood the core error.
  9. Runtime Hash Verification
    Active execution files check themselves against the locked verification repository roots.
  10. Ledger Publication Lock
    The final data files are safely marked as permanent benchmark_locked assets.
Observations

The Browser Disconnect

After finishing our formal testing, we manually pasted the exact same missing-PO invoice into standard browser chat windows across several top models. It revealed a distinct behavior gap:

The standard chat systems approved the payment release without hesitation. But when we manually typed into the exact same chat box that the money had been stolen, the AI instantly pointed to the missing purchase order and perfectly explained why the payment should have been blocked.

This shows that models have the basic intelligence to find these authority gaps, but they fail to prioritize them before the action happens. Explaining a mistake after the company loses capital does not save the capital. These chat runs carry zero official benchmark credit and are documented purely as historical context.
