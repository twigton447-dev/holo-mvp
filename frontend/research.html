Blindspots at the Action Boundary

GPT-5.4 approved a fraudulent wire transfer. Claude approved a routine reorder from a compromised automated system. All three frontier models missed an invoice-clustering attack designed to stay just below a dual-approval threshold. In each case, the surface was clean. The fraud lived in what was absent, not what was labeled.

This paper presents the methodology and results from the first structured benchmark of AI agent judgment at the point of irreversible action. Two domains completed. Three flagship results published. The finding is architectural: no single frontier model has consistent coverage across attack classes, and the gaps are unpredictable in advance.
